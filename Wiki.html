Dubai (Arabic: دبي) is a city and one of the seven emirates of the United Arab Emirates. Located on the southeastern coast of the Persian Gulf, Dubai grew from a small port on Dubai Creek into a global hub for business, aviation, and tourism. While early oil income funded infrastructure, today the economy is mainly services: trade, real estate, finance, logistics, and travel.

History

Communities around Dubai Creek practiced fishing, pearling, and trade with Persia and the Indian subcontinent. In the 20th century, low tariffs and dredging of the creek kept Dubai a busy entrepôt. Oil discovered in the 1960s funded roads, housing, and ports. From the 1990s, the emirate focused on aviation, logistics, tourism, and free zones.

Geography and climate

Dubai lies on a flat desert plain along the Persian Gulf. The coast features sandy beaches; inland are dunes and salt flats. Summers are very hot and humid (often above 40 °C), while winters are warm with light rainfall, mostly from November to March.

Government

Dubai is ruled by the Al Maktoum family and forms part of the federal UAE. Local authorities manage roads, planning, utilities, tourism, and media/technology zones.

Economy

Hydrocarbons are a small part of Dubai’s GDP today. Major sectors include:

  • Trade & logistics: Jebel Ali Port and free zone; global air links via DXB.
  • Tourism & hospitality: Leisure resorts, shopping, business travel.
  • Real estate & construction: Master-planned communities and mixed-use projects.
  • Finance & services: Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC).
  • Technology & media: Internet City and Media City clusters.

Demographics

Dubai is majority expatriate, with residents from South Asia, the Middle East, Africa, Europe, and East Asia. Arabic is official; English is widely used in business and daily life.

Culture

Emirati traditions—rooted in Bedouin life, seafaring, and Islam—sit alongside a multicultural lifestyle. Festivals, heritage areas by the Creek, and modern galleries and theatres are part of the city scene.

Landmarks and tourism

Popular sights include Burj Khalifa (world’s tallest building), Palm Jumeirah, Burj Al Arab, souqs near the Creek, and large malls such as Dubai Mall. Activities range from desert safaris to beaches and indoor attractions.

Transport

Dubai has a driverless metro, buses, trams, taxis, and marine transport. Dubai International Airport is among the busiest for international passengers.

Sports and events

Major events include the Dubai World Cup (horse racing), Dubai Tennis Championships, golf tournaments, and international trade fairs.
